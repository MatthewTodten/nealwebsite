Neal Todten

Neal Todten is a composer/artist based in New Orleans, LA working in abstract and experimental forms. In 2018 he began to produce concerts featuring contemporary classical music at spaces within the New Orleans DIY artist community. In doing so he found a space to freely explore experimental music with a new audience.

Neal Todten's music has taken on aspects of his often minimalist concert productions, but also incorporates his experience as an New Orleans musician. Improvisation, expressive melodic content, and a deep exploration of syncopation are combined with extended compositional forms and experimental techniques. A particular interest is placed on the intersection of composition and improvisation, finding new ways frame improvisation in performance.

He curates an annual contemporary music series in New Orleans, working as music director, producer, and composing new works for programs. Presentations have included performances of major works such as Music With Changing Parts – Philip Glass, Music for 18 Musicians – Steve Reich, In C – Terry Riley, and Coming Together – Fredric Rzweski. Concerts feature extensive visual works, with promotional design and staging backdrops by Neal Todten.

An avid proponent of independent arts practice, Neal Todten has developed original works in several mediums to incorporate into live performance; collaborating with dancers, artists, actors, and film makers. Neal Todten has produced several large scale paintings utilizing elements of cascading repetition to serve as a visual element for performances. Additionally he has choreographed dance for multiple dancers, and written spoken word narrative used in theatrical musical presentations. All of these pursuits are in an effort to present multi-disciplinary performances employing several mediums as an extension his musical composition.

In 2024 he collaborated with cinematographer A. Hennen Payne to produce the independent short film, House of Direction. The film is an experimental cinematic presentation of live performances developed for a multi-disciplinary exhibition of the same name, shot on 16mm b/w film.

Neal Todten has presented work at the Happyland Theater, Music Box Village, Mudlark Public Theater, AlterSpace Gallery, and the Living Gallery – Brooklyn. He has performed with Bread & Puppet Theater and worked with La Monte Young’s Dream House. He has worked as an accompanist for dance in New York City, New Orleans, and Cleveland.

He holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Music Composition from Cleveland State University, and has studied improvisation with pipe organist Paul Goussot, harmonization at the Academie International d’Ete de Nice, and received ballet accompanist training at the Princess Grace Academy in Monte-Carlo, Monaco.
